Andy Crossland Biography

 Andy Crossland is currently a news anchor and reporter for News 27 in Temecula, California, a CNN and AP affiliate. Prior to News 27, he has worked in San Diego, California, the 26th largest media market for major stations as a radio talk show host, television talk show host/producer, radio news and traffic reporter, and assignment editor for Fox 6 News, having the distinction of being a part of the number one ten o’clock news team in the May 2005 Nielsen ratings.

Andy has reported on many large breaking news events including the 2003 Cedar Fires which is the worst fire disaster in California history. He is very involved with his community and his profession and donates countless hours to hosting charity events and serving on various boards of directors including the San Diego Press Club, School of Digital and Media Design-San Diego City Schools, and is the past vice-president of the San Diego Chapter of the Society of Professional Journalists.

Andy is also a member of the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences and the Investigative Reporters and Editors Association. Prior to his broadcasting career, Andy was a police officer and businessman.
